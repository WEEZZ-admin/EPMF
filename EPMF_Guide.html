Part 1: Project Management（项目管理）

1.1 What is a Project?（什么是项目）

A project（项目） is a temporary endeavor（临时性工作） undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result（独特的产品、服务或成果）. Temporary means a defined start and end — not necessarily short. It produces a verifiable deliverable and drives change to create business value.

Portfolio（项目组合） → Program（项目集） → Project（项目） → Operations（运营）
  • Portfolio（项目组合）: Collection of programs, projects, sub-portfolios managed to achieve strategic objectives
  • Program（项目集）: Related projects managed in a coordinated way
  • Project（项目）: Temporary, unique, defined scope
  • Operations（运营）: Ongoing, repetitive activities

Business Value（商业价值）

Tangible（有形）
Monetary Assets
Stakeholder Equity
Utility, Fixtures, Tools
Market Share
Intangible（无形）
Goodwill, Brand recognition
Public benefit, Trademarks
Strategic alignment
Reputation

1.4 Project Selection Methods

Present Value
$$PV = \frac{FV}{(1+r)^n}$$
Benefit Cost Ratio
$$BCR = \frac{\text{Total Benefits}}{\text{Total Costs}}$$

BCR > 1 → project worth pursuing. BCR ≤ 1 → not worth it (unless intangible benefits exist). IRR — larger is better. NPV — positive indicates attractive project.

PDM — Four Dependency Types（前导图法-四种依赖类型）

TypeLogicExample
FS (most common)Successor cannot start until predecessor finishesAwards ceremony can't start until race finishes
FFSuccessor cannot finish until predecessor finishesInspection can't finish until painting finishes
SSSuccessor cannot start until predecessor startsPainting begins after priming starts
SF (least common)Successor cannot finish until predecessor startsFirst guard shift can't finish until second starts

Leads and Lags（提前与滞后）

  • Lead（提前）: Successor advances relative to predecessor (FS – 2 weeks)
  • Lag（滞后）: Successor is delayed (SS + 15 days)

PERT Three-Point Estimating（PERT三点估算）

Triangular Distribution
$$E = \frac{O + M + P}{3}$$
Beta Distribution (Traditional PERT)
$$E = \frac{O + 4M + P}{6}$$
Standard Deviation & Variance
$$\sigma = \frac{P - O}{6} \qquad \text{Variance} = \sigma^2$$

O = Optimistic, M = Most Likely, P = Pessimistic

Critical Path Method (CPM)（关键路径法）

The critical path（关键路径） is the longest path determining the minimum project duration. Calculated via forward pass and backward pass:

Forward Pass
$$EF = ES + D$$
Total Float (Slack)（总浮动时间）
$$\text{Total Float} = LF - EF = LS - ES$$

Activities on the critical path have zero total float.

Schedule Compression（进度压缩）

MethodHowCost
Crashing（赶工）Add resources to critical path activitiesIncreases cost
Fast Tracking（快速跟进）Run sequential activities in parallelIncreases risk

Both methods do NOT reduce project scope.

Earned Value Analysis (EVA)（挣值分析）

DimensionDefinition
PV（计划价值） (Planned Value)Authorized budget for scheduled work
EV（挣值） (Earned Value)Budget for work actually completed
AC（实际成本） (Actual Cost)Real money spent
BAC（完工预算）Total project budget
Schedule Variance（进度偏差）
$$SV = EV - PV$$

+ve = ahead; –ve = behind

Cost Variance（成本偏差）
$$CV = EV - AC$$

+ve = under budget; –ve = over budget

Schedule Performance Index（进度绩效指数）
$$SPI = \frac{EV}{PV}$$

>1 = ahead; <1 = behind

Cost Performance Index（成本绩效指数）
$$CPI = \frac{EV}{AC}$$

>1 = under budget; <1 = over budget

EVA Example

BAC = $1,000, Planned 50%, Actual 40%, AC = $550

PV = 50% × $1,000 = $500EV = 40% × $1,000 = $400AC = $550
SV = 400 – 500 = –$100 (behind)CV = 400 – 550 = –$150 (over budget)
SPI = 400/500 = 0.8 (behind)CPI = 400/550 ≈ 0.73 (over budget)
🔥 Negative CV is often difficult for the project to recover.

Part 2: Design For Manufacturing（面向制造的设计）

5.1 DFM Introduction（DFM简介）

Design For Manufacturing (DFM)（面向制造的设计）: Designing components for ease of manufacturing at lower cost. 70%–80% of production costs are determined by design decisions. DFM is most effective at the initial design stage.

Six Principles of DFM（DFM六大原则）

#PrincipleDescription
1Simplicity（简单性）Few parts, straightforward shapes
2Standardization（标准化）Use standard components and processes
3Tolerance（公差）Balance precision needs with manufacturing cost
4Material Selection（材料选择）Consider strength, durability, manufacturability
5Automation（自动化）Improve efficiency, reduce labor costs
6Process Integration（流程整合）Close collaboration: design + manufacturing + QC

Benefits of DFM（DFM的好处）

Lower manufacturing costs · Improved efficiency · Higher quality · Streamlined development · Better supply chain · Enhanced competitiveness · Regulatory compliance

5.2 Design for Sustainability (D4S)（可持续性设计）

Three Pillars（三大支柱）: Economic + Environmental + Social (NOT Technological!)

Linear Economy（线性经济） vs Circular Economy（循环经济）

Linear Economy（线性经济）Circular Economy
Take → Make → DisposeReintroduce used parts as raw materials
Highly unsustainableResource-efficient; reduces pollution

4Rs（4R原则）: Reduce（减少） · Reuse（重用） · Repair（修复） · Recycle（回收） (NOT Replace!)

Seven Sustainability Principles（七大可持续性原则）

  1. Dematerialization（去物质化） — reduce material/energy throughput (miniaturization)
  2. Modular Design（模块化设计） — reusable components across products
  3. Prefer Renewable Energy（偏好可再生能源） — wind, solar, hydroelectricity
  4. Product-Service Systems（产品服务系统） — lease instead of sell (e.g., IBM cloud)
  5. Design for Longevity（长寿设计） — maximize usable life
  6. Limit Long-Distance Outsourcing（限制长途外包） — prefer local suppliers
  7. Invest in Simulation（投资仿真） — replace trial-and-error with simulation
🔥 80% of a product's negative environmental impact is determined at the design stage. (EU Commission)

Control Chart Formulas（控制图公式）

Mean & Standard Deviation
$$\bar{X} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} x_i}{N} \qquad \hat{\sigma} = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x_i - \bar{X})^2}{N}}$$
Control Limits
$$UCL = \bar{X} + A\hat{\sigma} \qquad LCL = \bar{X} - A\hat{\sigma}$$

A=1 → 68.26%; A=2 → 95.44%; A=3 → 99.73%

7.1 Robust Design（稳健设计） (Taguchi Method)（田口方法）

Core idea: Increase quality by decreasing the effects of variation without eliminating the causes — since causes are often too difficult or expensive to control.

Developed by Genichi Taguchi (1950s–1960s).

Quality Loss Function（质量损失函数）

Traditional Step Function（传统阶跃函数）

L(y) = 0 if |y – m| < Δ₀; L(y) = A₀ otherwise — any value within tolerance is equally good.

Taguchi Quadratic Loss Function（田口二次损失函数）
Taguchi Loss Function
$$L(y) = k(y - m)^2 \qquad \text{where } k = \frac{A_0}{\Delta_0^2}$$

At y = m, loss = 0 (best). Loss increases slowly near m, then accelerates.

Average Quality Loss（平均质量损失）

For large n (nominal-the-best)
$$Q = k[(\mu - m)^2 + \sigma^2]$$

Three Types of Quality Characteristics（三类质量特性）

TypeIdeal TargetExamples
Nominal-the-best（望目特性）Specific target valueOperating temp, bandwidth, voltage
Smaller-the-better（望小特性）ZeroResponse time, leakage current, pollution
Larger-the-better（望大特性）InfinityEfficiency, accuracy, power amplification
🔥 "Target-the-zero" is NOT a Taguchi loss function type.

P-Diagram（参数图） — Three Parameter Classes（三类参数）

  1. Signal Factors（信号因子）: Direct impact on performance (voltage, frequency)
  2. Control Factors（控制因子）: Designer can specify optimally (process parameters, material)
  3. Noise Factors（噪声因子）: Cannot be controlled or too expensive (temperature, humidity, tolerances)

Taguchi Parameter Design（田口参数设计） — 5 Steps（5步）

  1. Brainstorming
  2. Identify design parameters & noise factors
  3. Construct DOE
  4. Perform experiments
  5. Analyze results

Design of Experiments (DOE)（实验设计）

Full Factorial
$$\text{\# Experiments} = (\text{\# levels})^{\text{\# factors}}$$

Orthogonal Arrays (OA)（正交数组）: L₄(2³) = 4 experiments, 2 levels, 3 variables. L₉(3⁴) = 9 experiments, 3 levels, 4 variables.

Crossed Arrays（交叉数组） (Inner（内数组） & Outer（外数组）)

  • Inner Array（内数组）: Control factor matrix
  • Outer Array（外数组）: Noise factor matrix
  • For each inner row, run all outer combinations → compute μ, σ², Q = k[(μ−m)²+σ²]
  • Select row with smallest loss function

8.2 Process Capability Indices（过程能力指数）

Cp — Potential Capability（潜在能力） (does NOT account for centering)
$$Cp = \frac{USL - LSL}{6\sigma}$$
Cpk — Actual Capability（实际能力） (accounts for spread AND centering)
$$Cpk = \min\left(\frac{USL - \mu}{3\sigma}, \frac{\mu - LSL}{3\sigma}\right)$$

Requirements: n ≥ 50, normally distributed data.

Cpk Risk Assessment（Cpk风险评估）

Cpk ValueRiskInterpretation
< 1.00HighProcess not capable
1.00 – 1.33ModerateImprovement needed
1.33 – 1.67LowProcess capable, room for improvement
≥ 1.67MinimalProcess highly capable
🔥 Aim for Cpk ≥ 2.00 — uses only 50% of spec width, significantly reducing defect risk.

Example

Resistor: target 10Ω, ±1Ω (USL=11, LSL=9). μ = 10.1Ω, σ = 0.3Ω.

  • Cp = 2 / (6×0.3) = 1.11
  • Upper: (11–10.1) / (3×0.3) = 1.00
  • Lower: (10.1–9) / (3×0.3) = 1.22
  • Cpk = min(1.00, 1.22) = 1.00 → Moderate risk

Operating · Investing · Financing activities. If Cash Outflows > Cash Inflows, the company cannot trade. "Cash Flow is THE life blood."

10.1 Make or Buy Decisions（自制或外购决策）

Only RELEVANT COSTS（相关成本） matter — costs that differ between alternatives. Fixed costs that cannot be avoided if buying externally are irrelevant.

Opportunity Cost（机会成本） Changes Everything

If idle facilities can be leased for $250K/year:

  • Make: $560K
  • Buy: $760K

11.1 Break-Even Analysis（盈亏平衡分析）

Contribution per Unit（单位贡献）
$$\text{Contribution} = SP - VC$$
Break-Even Point（盈亏平衡点） (units)
$$Q = \frac{FC}{SP - VC} = \frac{F}{\text{Contribution}}$$
Target Profit Output（目标利润产量）
$$Q = \frac{FC + \text{Target Profit}}{SP - VC}$$
Margin of Safety（安全边际）
$$\text{Margin of Safety} = \text{Current Output} - \text{BEP Output}$$
Break-Even Revenue（盈亏平衡收入） (Service Businesses)
$$\text{BE Revenue} = \frac{FC}{1 - (VC / SP)}$$

Three Methods（三种计算方法）

  1. Formula（公式法） (exact)
  2. Table（表格法） (organized data)
  3. Graphical（图示法） (visual: FC, TC, Revenue lines → intersection = BEP)

Applications（应用） & Limitations（局限性）

Uses: Marketing (price changes), Operations (new equipment), Location decisions, Price sensitivity.

Limitations: Costs/revenues not always linear; semi-variable costs exist; assumes all produced is sold; fixed costs may change.

13.2 Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)（折现现金流）

Time Value of Money（货币时间价值）: £1 today > £1 promised in the future — due to inflation, currency depreciation, and psychological preference.

Price（价格） = Cost（成本） + Profit（利润） (careful about pre-tax/post-tax).

DCF Formulas（DCF公式）

Single Payment — Compounding（复利）
$$F = P(1 + r)^n$$
Single Payment — Discounting（折现）
$$P = \frac{F}{(1 + r)^n}$$
Multiple Payments — Present Value（多笔支付现值）
$$PV = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{V_i}{(1 + r)^i}$$
Net Present Value（净现值）
$$NPV = PV_b - PV_c$$
Benefit/Cost Ratio（收益成本比）
$$B/C = \frac{NPV_b}{NPV_c} \qquad (\text{>1 → worth considering})$$
Internal Rate of Return（内部收益率）
$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{V_i}{(1 + IRR)^i} = 0$$
Equal Payment Series（等额支付系列）
$$F = A\frac{(1+i)^n - 1}{i}$$

DCF Analysis Steps（DCF分析步骤）

  1. Determine project lifetime (phone: 9–12mo, TV: 2yr, car: 5yr, power station: 50yr)
  2. Identify all cash flows (CapEx, OpEx, revenues) by year
  3. Select discount rate
  4. Calculate PV of each cash flow
  5. Sum to get NPV
  6. Calculate B/C ratio or IRR
🔥 Equivalent cash flows are equivalent at any common point in time. Equivalence depends on interest rate — you cannot compare amounts at different times without it.

14.1 Company Types（公司类型）

TypeKey Feature

Valuation（估值） & Share Dilution（股份稀释）

  • On formation day: Valuation = Sum of founder investments
  • Value grows through: IP creation, asset acquisition, sale of services
  • Market Capitalisation（市值） = Number of Shares × Share Price (opinion-based, can change overnight)
  • Share Dilution（股份稀释）: New investment increases total shares → original owners' percentage decreases. Raising share price based on new valuation reduces dilution.

16.1 Company Strategy（公司战略）

Strategy（战略） ≠ Vision（愿景）, Mission（使命）, Goals（目标）, Priorities（优先级）, Plans（计划）.
Strategy（战略） = Long-term advantage（长期优势） + Resource alignment（资源配置）.

"Know yourself and know your enemy, and you will never be defeated." — Sun Tzu, The Art of War

Three Fundamental Questions（三个基本问题） (Ken Favaro, Booz & Company)

  1. Who is the target customer?（谁是目标客户？）
  2. What is the value proposition to that customer?（对该客户的价值主张是什么？）
  3. What are the essential capabilities needed to deliver that value proposition?（交付该价值主张所需的关键能力是什么？）

Without clear and coherent answers, you don't have a strategy.

Strategy Levels Within a Company（公司内战略层次）

Product · Technology · Sales & Marketing · Manufacturing · Business Unit — all must fit together like a jigsaw. An effective organisation can only have ONE overall strategy.

Technology Strategy（技术战略） — Key Decisions

  • Leader or Follower?
  • Value of being first?
  • Which technology is important?
  • Do you need to own ALL technology?
  • IP Strategy: Own differentiating IP; license less important technology

🚨 HIGH-FREQUENCY EXAM TRAPS（高频易错点）

  1. PM spends ~90% of time communicating
  2. PM does NOT authorize business case → Sponsor does
  3. Standards = optional; Regulations = mandatory
  4. WBS = Work Breakdown Structure (NOT Statement)
  5. Cost baseline includes contingency, NOT management reserve
  6. Cost baseline is output of Determine Budget
  7. Max cost at Executing, NOT Initiating
  8. CPI > 1 = under budget (good); SPI < 1 = behind schedule
  9. Collaborating = best conflict resolution
  10. SWOT used in Identify Risks
  11. Probability & Impact Matrix used in Qualitative Risk Analysis
  12. Quantitative analysis NOT required for every project
  13. After Identify Risks → next is Qualitative (NOT Quantitative!)
  14. DFM most effective at initial design stage
  15. 3 Pillars: Economic + Environmental + Social (NOT Technological!)
  16. 4Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Repair, Recycle (NOT Replace!)
  17. DMAIC: NOT Calibrate
  18. Pareto = 80/20 rule
  19. External failure costs = WORST type of quality cost
  20. Equipment upgrades = Prevention cost (NOT Appraisal)
  21. Taguchi types: NOT "Target-the-zero"
  22. BCR ≤ 1 = not worth it (unless intangible benefits exist)
  23. Opportunity cost can flip Make-or-Buy decision
  24. Strategy ≠ Vision / Mission / Goals / Plans
  25. Structure should NOT be static — Strategy-Structure Fit is dynamic
  26. Equivalent cash flows are equivalent at any common point in time — equivalence depends on interest rate
